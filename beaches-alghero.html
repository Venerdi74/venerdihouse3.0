Best beaches in Alghero

A practical guide to help you choose where to go

Alghero and its beaches

If you are looking for the best beaches in Alghero and want to understand which ones to choose during your holiday, this guide can help you decide according to distance, comfort, atmosphere and the kind of beach day you want.

One of the reasons Alghero is so popular is that its coastline offers very different options within a relatively short distance. Some beaches are perfect for visitors who want to stay close to town, while others are better for those looking for wider views, crystal-clear water and a more natural setting. Knowing the difference helps you plan each day more easily and choose the beach that suits you best.

  • Beaches near the centre: Lido, Maria Pia
  • More scenic beaches: Le Bombarde, Lazzaretto
  • More natural settings: Mugoni, Porto Ferro

For anyone staying in town, this is one of the great advantages of Alghero: you can enjoy the sea without giving up the comfort of the city. If you are looking for a holiday home in Alghero near the sea, understanding the character of each beach makes it much easier to organise a more relaxed and enjoyable holiday.

Lido and Maria Pia: the most convenient beaches

Lido di Alghero is one of the easiest beaches to choose if you are staying in town. It is convenient, easy to reach and ideal for families, couples and anyone who wants to combine a few hours on the beach with a walk along the seafront. The shallow water also makes it a very comfortable option for visitors travelling with children.

Not far away, Maria Pia is one of the most loved beaches in the area thanks to its fine sand, clear water and beautiful pine forest behind it. It is an excellent choice for those who want to spend a long and comfortable day by the sea without going too far from Alghero.

Le Bombarde and Lazzaretto: the most iconic beaches

Le Bombarde is probably the most famous beach on the Alghero coast. The colours of the sea are among the most striking in the area, and the beach itself is one of the best known along the Riviera del Corallo. It is a great choice for travellers looking for a classic Sardinian beach experience with beautiful scenery and a lively atmosphere.

Close to Le Bombarde, Lazzaretto offers a slightly different feeling. The landscape is more varied, with smaller coves and a more intimate atmosphere. Many visitors choose it for its crystal-clear water, panoramic views and the slightly more sheltered feel compared with broader and busier beaches.

Mugoni and Porto Ferro: for nature and tranquillity

Mugoni has a calmer and more relaxed character. The wide bay, the surrounding greenery and the sense of space make it ideal for those who want a quieter beach day. It is especially appreciated by visitors who enjoy a slower rhythm and a more peaceful seaside setting.

Porto Ferro feels wilder and more open. It is less urban, more natural and often preferred by those who want to discover a different side of Alghero, away from the most classic town beaches. It is the right choice for anyone who loves broad views and a more untamed coastal atmosphere.

Which beach should you choose in Alghero?

If you want comfort and easy access from town, Lido and Maria Pia are among the best options, especially for families and for visitors who want to move around easily. If you are looking for spectacular sea colours and more iconic views, Le Bombarde and Lazzaretto are among the most famous choices. For a quieter and more natural setting, Mugoni and Porto Ferro are two of the best alternatives.

Choosing the right beach also depends on the kind of holiday you want: easy days close to the centre, more scenic outings with beautiful views, or quieter corners to enjoy at a slower pace. That is why staying in a practical part of Alghero can make the whole experience much easier.
