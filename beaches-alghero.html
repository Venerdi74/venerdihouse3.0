Best beaches in Alghero

A practical guide to help you choose where to go

Alghero and its beaches

If you are looking for the best beaches in Alghero and want to understand which ones to choose during your holiday, this guide can help you decide according to distance, atmosphere and type of beach.

One of the reasons Alghero is so popular is that its coastline offers very different options within a relatively short distance. Some beaches are close to the town centre and easy to reach, while others feel more scenic, quieter or more natural. This variety makes it much easier to plan each day depending on what kind of seaside experience you want.

  • Beaches near the centre: Lido, Maria Pia
  • More scenic beaches: Le Bombarde, Lazzaretto
  • More natural settings: Mugoni, Porto Ferro

For anyone staying in town, this is one of the great advantages of Alghero: you can enjoy the sea without giving up the comfort of the city. If you are looking for a holiday home in Alghero near the sea, knowing the character of each beach helps you organise your holiday in a much more relaxed way.

Lido and Maria Pia: the most convenient beaches

Lido di Alghero is one of the easiest beaches to choose if you are staying in town. It is convenient, easy to reach and ideal for anyone who wants to combine a few hours on the beach with a walk along the seafront or a quick return to the apartment. The shallow water also makes it particularly suitable for families.

Not far away, Maria Pia is one of the most loved beaches in the area. The fine sand, clear water and beautiful pine forest behind it create a setting that feels both bright and relaxing. It is an excellent choice for those who want to spend a long, comfortable day by the sea without going too far from Alghero.

Le Bombarde and Lazzaretto: the most iconic beaches

Le Bombarde is probably the most famous beach on the Alghero coast. The colours of the sea are among the most striking in the area, and the beach itself is one of the best known along the Riviera del Corallo. It is a great choice for those who want a classic Sardinian beach experience with beautiful views and a lively atmosphere.

Close to Le Bombarde, Lazzaretto offers a slightly different feeling. The landscape is more varied, with smaller coves and a more intimate atmosphere. Many visitors choose it for its crystal-clear water and for the more suggestive natural setting compared with broader, more open beaches.

Mugoni and Porto Ferro: for nature and tranquillity

Mugoni has a calmer and more relaxed character. The wide bay, the surrounding greenery and the sense of space make it ideal for those who want a quieter beach day. It is especially appreciated by visitors who enjoy a slower rhythm and softer landscapes.

Porto Ferro, on the other hand, feels wilder and more open. It is less urban, more natural and often preferred by those who want to discover a different side of Alghero, away from the most classic town beaches. It is the right choice for anyone who loves broad views and a more untamed coastal atmosphere.

Which beach should you choose in Alghero?

If you want comfort and easy access from town, Lido and Maria Pia are among the best options. If you are looking for spectacular sea colours and more iconic views, Le Bombarde and Lazzaretto are among the most famous choices. For a quieter and more natural setting, Mugoni and Porto Ferro are two of the best alternatives.

In the end, this is exactly what makes Alghero so appealing: there is not just one right beach, but several different options that suit different kinds of holidays.
